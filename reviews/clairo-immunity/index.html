Immunity
Clairo
2019
7.4


[TL;DR]
Like a soft gust of wind, Clairo's Immunity touches on many emotions but leaves everything upright as it weaves through the people, places, and things in her life.

Clairo's Immunity is a quietly confident exploration of indie pop's emotional range. Tracks like "Bags" and "Alewife" balance lo-fi charm with polished production, where her soft vocals cut through like a cool breeze. The introspective lyrics touch on love, identity, and vulnerability, but without wallowing in self-pity. A little fragile, a little grounded, it's a triumph in low-key tone and texture.

Some songs on Immunity are, in fact, susceptible to a few questionable choices. "White Flag" sees Clairo sing over an engagingly sidechained, almost-hip-hop beat that unfortunately outshines her lyrics. On "Impossible", she sings through a processed filter mismatched with her musical identity. And in the middle of the song "Sofia", a growling bass at the minute-and-a-half mark drains the song of its amiable sapphic vibe she'd been cultivating over a mellow disco beat.

Favorite song: "Alewife"
