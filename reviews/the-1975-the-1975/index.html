[TL;DR]
Where substance costs twenty bucks, the 1975 comes up short. Not to worry, however, since the quartet always has a shiny trick up its sleeve.

If I had to describe the 1975's debut album in one word, it'd be "shiny". They're evidently very ambitious, blending '80s synth-pop, post-punk, and ambient electronica into something that absolutely works. It's the musical equivalent of throwing a bunch of cool stuff in a blender and pressing "pulse" once. The band straddles contradictions: melancholy and celebration, atmospheric and brash, like the soundtrack to a night out in a dimly lit, half-empty nightclub where everyone's too cool to have fun.

What's truly impressive is how natural the 1975 makes their stuff sound. The album exudes a carefully crafted mood that's just pretentious enough to make you feel like you're in on some cool secret, or on the verge of some life-changing destiny you're just waiting for to happen.
