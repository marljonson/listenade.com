TL;DR:
Blank Banshee 0 meshes '90s sound effects with '10s trap to produce a commendably atmospheric debut effort.

There's a hypnotic quality to Blank Banshee 0. It sounds both futuristic and retro, seamlessly meshing smooth synths and trap beats. The album has an eerie calmness, as if the listener was drifting in zero gravity across space, recalling in distant fragments the clicks and beeps of 1990s computer software.

Released digitally for free in 2012 by Patrick Driscoll (his real name), Blank Banshee 0 was a pioneer in atmospheric trap music, like his contemporary Clams Casino. Driscoll predicted the emergence of cloud rap, an ethereal-sounding hip hop subgenre characterized by the hazy, dream-like undertones of ambient music (see "I'm God" by Oakland rapper Lil B).

At its core, "vaporwave" music is a critique of Western consumer culture in the 1980s and '90s, or at least a distortion of that culture. Driscoll's Blank Banshee 0 presents a minimalistic soundscape considered foreign to popular music in 2012. Driscoll injects into its veins the newness of the early digital landscape in which he grew up, and it makes for good, spacey time-travel music.

Favorite song: "Ammonia Clouds"
